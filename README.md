# GTM Brain

**A portable, evidence-backed methodology for building B2B commercial engines.**

10 interconnected tenets. Operational tools. A systematic approach to evaluating, architecting, and executing go-to-market motions — from zero to scale.

---

## What This Is

This is not a framework written on a whiteboard or borrowed from a book. It was built from building the commercial layer at companies that didn't have one — then extracted, codified, and pressure-tested through structured analysis.

The methodology is organized into 10 tenets that form a system, not a checklist. A weak ICP poisons positioning, messaging, outbound, and pipeline quality. A missing sales process means nothing downstream is consistent. The tenets are how you think about GTM. The tools and playbooks in this repo are what you use.

**Career throughline:** ClearSlide (pre-Series A, hyper-growth) → TubeMogul (three progressive roles through Adobe acquisition) → Acceleration Labs (productized GTM for 12+ founders, build-operate-transfer) → Adobe (enterprise at scale) → Hudson MX (VP Sales, $17M ARR) → Capsule.video (zero to $2M ARR, $12M Series A).

**Operating model:** Builder, not optimizer. Player-coach, not pure manager. Start with product truth, not market opportunity. Create the category, don't compete in one. Diagnose before prescribing. Name the constraint, don't list all the problems.

## The 10 Tenets

| # | Tenet | What It Answers |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **ICP & Market Segmentation** | Who are you selling to, ranked by fit? Firmographic, technographic, and behavioral signals that define your best buyers. Without this, everything downstream is guesswork. |
| 2 | **Value Proposition & Positioning** | Why you, why now, why over alternatives? Not your feature list — the specific outcome you deliver for a specific buyer in a specific context. |
| 3 | **Buyer Journey & Sales Motion** | How buyers actually make decisions vs. how you want to sell to them. Maps what the buyer needs to believe, decide, and do at each stage — not just what the seller does. |
| 4 | **Discovery & Qualification** | How you identify real opportunities vs. noise. Intentional curiosity over mechanical interrogation. A five-step sequence where the order is the insight. |
| 5 | **Messaging by Persona** | The specific language for each stakeholder in the buying committee. Same product, different emphasis — practitioner hears workflow impact, VP hears team efficiency, CFO hears cost displacement. |
| 6 | **Outbound & Demand Gen** | How pipeline gets created. Three-layer philosophy: outbound is precision, inbound is signal, marketing is the amplifier. Includes PLG demand gen — harvest before you hunt. |
| 7 | **Sales Process & Playbook** | Stage-by-stage operating system with exit criteria, inspection questions, and forecast methodology. Built from scratch at 6+ companies, productized for 12+. |
| 8 | **Pricing & Packaging** | The full pricing model landscape — seat-based, usage-based, consumption, hybrid commit, outcome-based. Value metric selection, packaging architecture, discounting discipline. |
| 9 | **Metrics & Pipeline Hygiene** | The numbers that tell you if the engine is working. SQO-anchored dashboards, pipeline arithmetic, three-tier forecasting, and diagnostic frameworks that connect metric movements to upstream tenet breakdowns. |
| 10 | **Enablement & Onboarding** | How you replicate what works. Readiness-based ramp, multi-mode coaching, minimum viable enablement artifacts, and knowledge transfer as a system. |

## How the Tenets Connect

The tenets aren't independent — they form a system:

- **Stakeholder Framework** (Tenet 1) → one facilitated exercise produces ICP, positioning inputs (Tenet 2), and persona messaging (Tenet 5) simultaneously. Applied at 12+ companies.
- **ICP clarity** (Tenet 1) → determines outbound targeting (Tenet 6), discovery focus (Tenet 4), and pipeline quality (Tenet 9).
- **Category creation** (Tenet 2) → creates pricing integrity (Tenet 8) and protects the sales process (Tenet 7) from commodity comparisons.
- **Discovery** (Tenet 4) → qualification signals become CRM fields (Tenet 9), which drive pipeline reviews and forecasting.
- **Technical/business validation separation** (Tenet 3) → structures the deal arc and determines champion-to-executive escalation timing (Tenet 5).
- **Sales process** (Tenet 7) → the operating system that everything else runs on.

When one tenet breaks, the damage is never contained to that tenet. The system connection map is how you diagnose where the real problem is vs. where the symptoms show up.

## How This Was Built

This methodology was systematically extracted from real GTM execution data and pressure-tested through structured analysis.

**Data corpus:**
- 1,007 call transcripts (691 Tactiq + 316 Gong)
- 292 commercial sales decks
- 761 GTM-relevant documents (from 46,000 enumerated in Google Drive)
- Full CRM: 1,175 closed-won + 1,048 closed-lost deals, 50+ outbound sequences
- 6 months of sent/received email
- Google Calendar history

**Extraction process:** Seven structured analysis jobs, each with a specific lens — Voice & Communication Style (48,803 speaking turns), Sales Methodology & Execution (50,021 utterances, 21,451 pattern instances), GTM Strategy & Operator Thinking (12,674 strategic utterances), Revenue Growth & Playbook (104 patterns across 7 categories), Leadership & Influence Style (3,865 matches), Gap Analysis (10 tenets scored, two independent lenses), and Identity & Philosophy (capstone synthesis — 10 beliefs codified with cross-company evidence).

**Validation:** Frameworks were tested against real deals. Buyer-side journey framework validated against 15 deals. Disqualification framework tested against 19 deals with zero false positives on won deals. Gap analysis applied two independent lenses — a maturity rubric and a simulated CEO exposure test.

**Cross-company evidence:** The methodology draws from experience across ClearSlide, TubeMogul, Acceleration Labs (12+ founder engagements), Adobe, Hudson MX, and Capsule.video. The Stakeholder Framework has been applied at 12+ companies with completed examples from three different markets.

**What makes it different from a textbook:** Every principle was observed in practice before it was codified. The discovery sequence was mapped from 50,021 real utterances, not prescribed from a training manual. The pricing conviction framework came from 1,113 pricing instances and 849 negotiation patterns. The methodology earns credibility by being specific about where the evidence comes from.

## How to Use This

**Evaluating a company's GTM motion:** Start with the [Company Assessment Template](tools/company_assessment_template.md). It walks through company profile collection, red flag scanning, tenet-by-tenet scoring, and cross-tenet synthesis. Outputs a GTM assessment, 90-day plan, or gap identification.

**Building a PLG-to-enterprise motion:** Start with the [PLG-to-Enterprise Conversion Playbook](playbooks/plg_to_enterprise_conversion.md), then use the four companion tools (scoring card, dashboard spec, first touch playbook, committed spend framework) as operational artifacts for execution.

**Diagnosing a stalled pipeline:** Use the [Buyer-Side Diagnostic Checklist](tools/buyer_side_diagnostic_checklist.md) to identify where deals are stuck from the buyer's perspective. Then trace backward through the tenets — is it a discovery problem (Tenet 4), a positioning problem (Tenet 2), or a buyer journey navigation problem (Tenet 3)?

**Running a deal post-mortem:** Use the [Deal Post-Mortem Template](tools/deal_post_mortem_template.md) to analyze individual losses, then aggregate quarterly to identify systemic patterns.

**Qualifying out faster:** Use the [Negative ICP Checklist](tools/negative_icp_checklist.md) to codify disqualification patterns. Every bad-fit deal in the pipeline consumes selling time, distorts forecasts, and demoralizes the team.

## Core Beliefs

1. **Start with product truth, not market opportunity.** Understand what the product does well today — not aspirationally — and work outward from there.
2. **Create the category, don't compete in one.** Position as something new rather than something better. Eliminates competitors, creates pricing integrity.
3. **The GTM flywheel connects vision, marketing, sales, and product.** The GTM leader makes it spin. When it breaks, the whole system degrades.
4. **Intentional curiosity drives discovery.** Not a checklist — genuine curiosity about how buyers work, what's broken, and what they actually need.
5. **Buyer empathy is a selling advantage.** Understand how buyers actually make decisions. Deals die in the buyer's organization, not the seller's pipeline.
6. **Pricing confidence comes from value conviction.** If you've proven value, hold the price. Discounting signals you don't believe in what you sell.
7. **Experimentation culture beats best practices.** Test, measure, learn. The best playbook is one that evolves with data.
8. **Coaching builds judgment, not dependence.** Socratic first — ask reps to diagnose before prescribing. Build thinkers, not followers.
9. **Methodology is portable.** The 10 tenets transfer across companies. Evidence and specifics change; the thinking architecture doesn't.
10. **Build-Operate-Transfer.** Build the system, operate it to prove it works, transfer it to the team so it runs without you.
