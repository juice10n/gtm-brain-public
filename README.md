# GTM Brain

**A portable, evidence-backed methodology for building B2B commercial engines.**

10 interconnected tenets. Operational tools. A systematic approach to evaluating, architecting, and executing go-to-market motions, from zero to scale. Extracted from 1,000+ call transcripts, 292 sales decks, 2,200+ CRM deals, and 15 years of GTM execution data across 6 companies.

---

## What This Is

This is not a framework written on a whiteboard or borrowed from a book. It was extracted from real execution, codified into a system, and pressure-tested through structured analysis.

The methodology is organized into 10 tenets that form a system, not a checklist. They're interconnected: a weak ICP poisons positioning, messaging, outbound, and pipeline quality. A missing sales process breaks everything downstream. The tenets are the thinking. The tools and playbooks in this repo are the execution.

The methodology was extracted through seven structured analysis jobs, each applied to a different dimension of the data:

| Job | Scope |
|---|---|
| Voice & Communication Style | 48,803 speaking turns |
| Sales Methodology & Execution | 50,021 utterances, 21,451 pattern instances |
| GTM Strategy & Operator Thinking | 12,674 strategic utterances |
| Revenue Growth & Playbook | 104 patterns across 7 categories |
| Leadership & Influence Style | 3,865 matches |
| Gap Analysis | 10 tenets scored, two independent lenses |
| Identity & Philosophy | Capstone synthesis, 10 beliefs codified with cross-company evidence |

## The 10 Tenets

**1. ICP & Market Segmentation:**
Who should you be selling to, and in what order? Use case first, firmographics second. A company can match every filter on paper and still be a terrible customer if there's no recurring workflow behind the purchase.

**2. Value Proposition & Positioning:**
Why anything, why you, why now? Not your feature list. The specific outcome you deliver for a specific buyer in a specific context. Category creation beats competitive positioning every time.

**3. Buyer Journey & Sales Motion:**
How do buyers actually make decisions, and where does your process break from theirs? The buyer journey and the sales process are different things. Conflating them is the most common reason deals stall.

**4. Discovery & Qualification:**
How do you separate real opportunities from noise? A five-step sequence where the order matters. Workflow first, budget last. When discovery feels incomplete, the sequence itself tells you which step was skipped.

**5. Messaging by Persona:**
How do you say the same thing differently to each person in the buying committee? Practitioner hears workflow impact, VP hears team efficiency, CFO hears cost displacement. Same product, different emphasis.

**6. Outbound & Demand Gen:**
How does pipeline get created? Three complementary layers: outbound is precision, inbound is signal, marketing is the amplifier. Most GTM leaders treat these as substitutes. They're multipliers.

**7. Sales Process & Playbook:**
What has to be true before a deal moves forward? Stage progression should be driven by buyer behavior, not seller activity. When deals advance because the rep checked a box instead of because the buyer took action, the pipeline becomes fiction.

**8. Pricing & Packaging:**
How do you price with confidence? Anchor against the cost of the buyer's current alternative, not competitors. Category creation protects this: no direct benchmark means value-based pricing without commodity pressure.

**9. Metrics & Pipeline Hygiene:**
How do you know if the engine is working? Metrics are diagnostic, not performative. A dropping win rate doesn't tell you to "improve the win rate." It tells you to look upstream at qualification, positioning, or the buyer journey.

**10. Enablement & Onboarding:**
How do you replicate what works? Coaching and enablement are different disciplines. Coaching without enablement doesn't scale. Enablement without coaching doesn't stick. The team ends up with knowledge they can't apply and instincts they can't explain.

### Playbooks

Cross-tenet integration documents that show the full motion end-to-end.

**[PLG-to-Enterprise Conversion](playbooks/plg_to_enterprise_conversion.md):**

Covers account scoring, the PLG buyer journey, adapted discovery, champion-to-executive messaging, consumption-to-committed pricing, PLG-specific metrics, and a 90-day build sequence.

Companion tools:

- **[Account Scoring Card](tools/plg_account_scoring_card.md):** Score a self-serve account's readiness for sales engagement in under two minutes. Five signal categories, negative PQA filter, tier placement.
- **[Inbound Conversion Dashboard Spec](tools/plg_inbound_conversion_dashboard_spec.md):** Six-metric PLG dashboard with data sources, visualization format, and alert thresholds. Ready to hand to ops for implementation.
- **[First Touch Playbook](tools/plg_first_touch_playbook.md):** Three outreach scenarios for PLG accounts with engagement triggers and talk tracks.
- **[Committed Spend Proposal Framework](tools/plg_committed_spend_proposal_framework.md):** Structure for building consumption-to-committed offers. Floor setting, discount framing, overage handling, and three commitment structures.

### Tools

Standalone operational artifacts. Each one applies a specific tenet to a specific moment in the GTM motion.

- **[Company Assessment Template](tools/company_assessment_template.md)** (All 10 Tenets): Structured intake framework for evaluating any company's GTM motion. Red flag scan, tenet-by-tenet scoring, cross-tenet synthesis. Outputs a GTM assessment, 90-day plan, or gap identification.
- **[Negative ICP Checklist](tools/negative_icp_checklist.md)** (Tenet 1): Disqualification patterns: the characteristics, behaviors, and deal shapes that reliably predict failure. When to stop pursuing a deal.
- **[Buyer Enablement Template](tools/buyer_enablement_template.md)** (Tenet 3): The artifact you give your champion to help them navigate their own organization's buying process. Not a product sheet, a navigation guide.
- **[Buyer-Side Diagnostic Checklist](tools/buyer_side_diagnostic_checklist.md)** (Tenet 3): Pipeline review tool for diagnosing where a deal is stuck from the buyer's perspective, not the seller's.
- **[Deal Post-Mortem Template](tools/deal_post_mortem_template.md)** (Tenet 9): Structured loss analysis with root cause taxonomy. Turns individual deal losses into systemic insights.

## Core Beliefs

**Diagnose before prescribing.** Having a system doesn't mean running the same playbook everywhere. Understand the current state first — what's working, what's broken, and where the real constraint is. The methodology is a lens, not a script.

**Start with product truth, not market opportunity.** Understand what the product does well today and where the vision is heading, then build the GTM outward from that intersection.

**Create the category, don't compete in one.** Position as something new rather than something better. Eliminates competitors, creates pricing integrity.

**Work the system, not the tenet.** The best exercises produce outputs across multiple tenets simultaneously. The Stakeholder Framework is the proof — one facilitated session feeds ICP, positioning, and persona messaging.

**Build-Operate-Transfer.** Build the system, operate it to prove it works, transfer it to the team so it runs without you.
