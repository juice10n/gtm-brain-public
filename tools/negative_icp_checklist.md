# Negative ICP Checklist
*Companion to the Stakeholder Framework — who you're NOT for*

**Source:** Capsule HubSpot loss taxonomy (1,048 closed-lost deals) + Tenet 4 disqualification framework

**How to use:** If a prospect exhibits 3+ of these signals, deprioritize or disqualify. This checklist should be reviewed in weekly pipeline reviews and applied before deals advance past discovery stage.

---

## The 7 Disqualification Signals

### 1. No Recurring Use Case
The prospect wants a one-off deliverable, not a repeatable workflow. They need one video, one campaign, one project — not a platform for ongoing production. **This was the #1 pattern in the 35% unqualified cohort at Capsule.** One-off buyers generate revenue but not retention.

*Diagnostic question:* "How often would you need to produce this type of content? Is this a one-time project or an ongoing need?"

### 2. No Current-State Cost
The prospect is not spending meaningful time, money, or headcount on the problem today. No agency spend, no dedicated headcount, no manual process consuming hours. If there's no current cost, there's no ROI anchor — every price feels expensive and there's no urgency to change.

*Diagnostic question:* "How are you handling this today? Who does it and how long does it take?"

### 3. Single User, No Team Structure
The prospect is an individual who wants the tool for personal use. No team to expand into, no organizational workflow to embed in. The deal ceiling is one seat with no expansion path. Per-seat economics don't justify the sales effort.

*Diagnostic question:* "Who else on your team would use this? How many people touch this workflow?"

### 4. Wrong Persona — No Organizational Influence
The contact loves the product but has no budget authority, no executive access, and no political capital to champion a purchase. This accounted for 10% of Capsule's closed-lost (wrong POC) and contributed significantly to the 25% ghosting rate — champions who couldn't navigate their own buying process.

*Diagnostic question:* "If you decided this was the right solution, what would the approval process look like? Who else would need to be involved?"

### 5. Commodity Buyer — Differentiation Doesn't Matter
The prospect's requirements are entirely met by cheaper alternatives. They don't care about your unique differentiator — they want the category's table-stakes features at the lowest price. You will compete on price, and you will either lose or win unprofitably.

*Diagnostic question:* "What's most important to you in evaluating options? What would make you choose one solution over another?"

### 6. No Active Initiative or Timeline
The prospect is "exploring" with no budget cycle, no executive mandate, no project timeline, and no compelling event. This was the 15% "bad timing" cohort at Capsule. These are future opportunities, not current pipeline.

*Diagnostic question:* "Is there a specific timeline you're working toward? What's driving the urgency to look at this now?"

### 7. Three Meaningful Outreach Attempts with No Response
The prospect was actively engaged and has gone dark. Three re-engagement attempts — each carrying specific value, not generic check-ins — have gone unanswered. The deal is dead or dormant. Move to nurture track.

*Diagnostic question:* N/A — this is a behavioral signal, not a conversation-based one. The diagnostic is: did each outreach add value or change the angle?

---

## How to Apply

**In pipeline reviews:** For every deal at Stage 2 or later, check against these 7 signals. If 3+ are present, the deal should be moved to nurture or disqualified.

**At deal creation:** Signals 1-3 should be assessed before a deal enters the pipeline. If all three are present, the deal doesn't enter.

**The conversation with the rep:** Frame disqualification as diagnostic skill, not failure. "What did you learn that told you this wasn't real?" makes pruning heroic, not shameful.
