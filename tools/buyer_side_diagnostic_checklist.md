# Buyer-Side Diagnostic Checklist
*Use in weekly pipeline reviews to diagnose where the buyer is in THEIR process*

**Purpose:** The seller-side CRM shows where the deal is in YOUR process. This checklist diagnoses where the deal is in the BUYER's process. When these two tracks diverge — when you've moved to proposal but the buyer hasn't achieved internal alignment — the deal stalls.

**How to use:** For every deal at Stage 2 or later, run through the diagnostic for the buyer-side stage you believe the deal is in. If the buyer-side stage is behind the seller-side stage, that's the problem to solve — not more selling activity.

---

## Stage 1: Awareness → Interest
*Buyer realizes they have a problem worth solving*

**Diagnostic signals (check all that apply):**
- [ ] Prospect engaged with educational content, event, or accepted outbound meeting
- [ ] Questions are about the problem space, not about vendors
- [ ] Someone internally has identified the problem and started exploring

**Red flags — buyer is NOT at this stage:**
- [ ] Meeting was taken as a favor, not from genuine interest
- [ ] No articulated pain or problem — "just exploring"
- [ ] No internal sponsor has raised this as a priority

**Pipeline review question:** *"Does this prospect have a real problem they're trying to solve, or are they just being polite?"*

---

## Stage 2: Evaluation
*Buyer explores whether your solution fits*

**Diagnostic signals (check all that apply):**
- [ ] Prospect asks detailed functionality questions specific to their workflow
- [ ] They describe their current process and ask how it would change
- [ ] They introduce you to additional stakeholders
- [ ] Questions shift from "How does this work?" to "How would this work for us?"

**Red flags — buyer is NOT at this stage:**
- [ ] Only one person engaged, no stakeholders introduced
- [ ] Questions remain generic, not tied to their workflow
- [ ] IT/security concerns raised that champion can't address

**Pipeline review question:** *"Has the buyer started evaluating us against their specific use case, or are we still in 'interesting idea' territory?"*

---

## Stage 3: Technical Validation
*Buyer confirms the product works*

**Diagnostic signals (check all that apply):**
- [ ] Users are actively engaged in pilot/POC (not just signed up)
- [ ] Champion asks about scaling beyond the pilot group
- [ ] Questions shift from "can it do X?" to "how would we roll this out?"
- [ ] Success criteria exist (even informal)
- [ ] Scheduled check-in exists to assess progress

**Red flags — buyer is NOT at this stage:**
- [ ] Users signed up but aren't using the product
- [ ] No success criteria defined — no way to declare "this worked"
- [ ] Champion too busy to drive internal adoption
- [ ] IT security review blocking access with no clear timeline

**Pipeline review question:** *"Are the pilot users actually using the product, and does someone on their side own the outcome?"*

---

## Stage 4: Business Validation
*Buyer confirms it's worth buying*

**Diagnostic signals (check all that apply):**
- [ ] Conversation shifted from functionality to business impact
- [ ] Champion asking for ROI data, case studies with dollar figures, comparison data
- [ ] Decision-maker has entered the conversation (directly or via champion relay)
- [ ] Budget discussion has started — not just "what does it cost?" but "how do we fund this?"

**Red flags — buyer is NOT at this stage:**
- [ ] Champion is convinced but hasn't talked to leadership
- [ ] No business case exists — champion can't articulate ROI
- [ ] Decision-maker sees it as "nice to have," not "must have"
- [ ] Budget is allocated elsewhere with no path to reallocation

**Pipeline review question:** *"Has the champion built a business case, and has someone with budget authority seen it?"*

---

## Stage 5: Commitment
*Buyer navigates internal procurement*

**Diagnostic signals (check all that apply):**
- [ ] Conversations focus on logistics: terms, billing, timeline, onboarding
- [ ] Procurement or legal contacts introduced
- [ ] Questions are about risk mitigation and compliance, not product capability
- [ ] Implementation planning discussions have started

**Red flags — buyer is NOT at this stage:**
- [ ] Procurement introduced but champion has disengaged
- [ ] Legal redlines with no executive escalation path
- [ ] Budget cycle doesn't align with deal timing
- [ ] Deal deprioritized due to competing initiatives

**Pipeline review question:** *"Is procurement actively working on this, or is it sitting in a queue with no internal champion pushing it?"*

---

## The Divergence Diagnostic

**When seller-side stage > buyer-side stage, ask:**

| Seller Stage | But Buyer Is At... | The Real Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Demo / Proposal | Stage 1 (Awareness) | They don't have a real problem yet. Stop selling. Start discovering. |
| Pilot / Negotiation | Stage 2 (Evaluation) | They haven't committed to evaluating seriously. The pilot will get low adoption. |
| Proposal / Close | Stage 3 (Technical) | Product works but no business case. Champion can't sell internally. Arm them. |
| Negotiation / Close | Stage 4 (Business) | Business case approved but procurement is a separate workstream. Treat it as such. |
