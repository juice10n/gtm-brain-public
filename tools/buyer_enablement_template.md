# Buyer Enablement Template
*A one-page guide for your champion to navigate their own buying process*

**Purpose:** Give this to your champion after they're convinced but before they start selling internally. It maps the steps between "I want this" and "the contract is signed."

**How to use:** Customize the fields below for each deal. Share with the champion as a collaborative document — a navigation guide, not a marketing piece.

### Step 1: Internal Alignment (Typical: 1-2 weeks)
**What happens:** 2-3 stakeholders agree this is worth evaluating.

**Who typically needs to know:**
- [ ] Your manager / budget holder: _______________
- [ ] End users who would use it: _______________
- [ ] IT / Security (if required): _______________

**Common blocker:** Stakeholders haven't seen it and don't understand the problem.

**We can provide:** 2-minute executive summary to forward, recorded demo, brief call with any stakeholder.

---

### Step 2: Technical Validation (Typical: 1-2 weeks for POC, 2-4 months for pilot)
**What happens:** Confirm the product works for your use case, with your data.

**Options:**
- [ ] Live demo tailored to your use case
- [ ] Hands-on POC (1-2 weeks, measurable outcome)
- [ ] Structured pilot (2-4 months, defined success criteria)

**Common blocker:** IT security review takes longer than expected. Pilot users don't engage.

**We can provide:** Pre-completed security questionnaire, SOC 2 report, AI governance docs. Onboarding support. Weekly check-ins.

---

### Step 3: Business Case (Typical: 1-2 weeks)
**What happens:** Build the justification for the investment for your leadership.

**Your business case should answer:**
- What is this costing us today? (agency, headcount, manual hours)
- What changes if we adopt? (time saved, cost reduced, output increased)
- What's the ROI over 12 months?

**Common blocker:** You're convinced but don't know how to frame it for leadership.

**We can provide:** Pre-built business case with your pilot/POC data. ROI calculator with your numbers. Case study from a company like yours. Executive-to-executive call if helpful.

---

### Step 4: Procurement & Legal (Typical: 2-4 weeks)
**What happens:** Contract review, legal terms, security approval, budget allocation.

**Who typically gets involved:**
- [ ] Procurement: _______________
- [ ] Legal: _______________
- [ ] IT Security: _______________
- [ ] Finance / Budget approval: _______________

**Common blocker:** Procurement has different priorities than you (risk mitigation, not product value). Legal redlines take multiple rounds.

**We can provide:** Standard contract ready for review. Security documentation package. Flexible billing options (monthly, quarterly, annual). Responsive turnaround on redlines — we aim for 24-48 hours.

---

### Step 5: Go Live
**What happens:** Contract signed, onboarding scheduled, implementation begins.

**We provide:** Dedicated onboarding plan, implementation timeline, training for your team, ongoing support contact.
